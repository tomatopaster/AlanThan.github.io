What does it do?

Our product is a model that informs the user whether the North American Gilled Mushroom is poisonous or edible based on the description of the mushroom's traits.

It is established on the dataset from Kaggle, it's information of mushrooms is represented as letters, examples are 'p' = poisonous, 'd' = descending, or 'g' = grooves, and it's considered as a classification problem. Before we changed the letters into integers for a better understanding, we decided to convert them into decimals between 0 and 1 instead.

To solve the North American Gilled Mushroom problem, we created four algorithms that can help determined the mushroom's type, such as the KNN (k-nearest neighbor) model, the SVM (support vector machines) model, the Random Forest model, and the Neural Network model.

Conclusion

Based on the machine learning models we have used for this project, we discovered that all four algorithms perform well for our data set, making them viable. However, due to the Neural Network's programming taking too long, it would be recommended to use the other three algorithms we have on the list to save time.

When each algorithm observed the dataset of mushrooms solely, we found out that the majority of them use odor, spore print color, gill color, gill size, ring type, and bruises as the most important features to identify a mushroom. The purpose of this study is to encourage mushroom foragers and to shrink the possibility of consuming poisonous mushrooms.
